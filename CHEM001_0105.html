1. Measurement in Chemistry

Chemistry is a quantitative science, meaning it relies heavily on measurements. Scientists use measurements to describe properties such as mass, volume, temperature, and time.

Key Idea: Reliable measurements are essential for accurate scientific conclusions.

A graduated cylinder is used to measure liquid volume accurately.

Quick Check

Why are measurements important in chemistry?

They allow scientists to quantify observations and make reliable conclusions.

2. Accuracy vs Precision

Two important concepts in measurement are accuracy and precision.

  • Accuracy: how close a measurement is to the true value
  • Precision: how close repeated measurements are to each other
Key Idea: Measurements can be precise without being accurate, and vice versa.

3. Uncertainty and Errors

All measurements contain some level of uncertainty due to limitations of instruments or human observation.

  • Systematic error: consistent bias in measurement
  • Random error: unpredictable variation
Key Idea: No measurement is perfectly exact—understanding error improves reliability.

Challenge Problem

Why is it important to minimize systematic errors in experiments?

Because systematic errors shift all measurements away from the true value, making results consistently inaccurate.
