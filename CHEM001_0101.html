Learning Objectives

1. What Is Matter Made Of?

All matter is composed of extremely small particles—atoms, molecules, or ions. These particles are not visible to the naked eye, but their behavior explains everything from the smell of perfume spreading in a room to the expansion of gases.

Key Idea: Matter is made of tiny particles that are separated by space.

Particles in solids, liquids, and gases differ in spacing and arrangement.

Quick Check

Is matter continuous or made of particles?

Matter is made of discrete particles, not continuous.

2. Motion of Particles

Particles are in constant random motion. This motion is directly related to temperature. The higher the temperature, the faster the particles move.

Key Idea: Temperature measures the average kinetic energy of particles.

Visualization of particle motion in different states of matter.

Concept Check

Why do substances expand when heated?

Heating increases particle kinetic energy, causing particles to move faster and spread apart.

3. Particle Interactions and Space

Particles interact through attractive forces. These forces vary depending on how close the particles are.

  • Strong forces → solids
  • Moderate forces → liquids
  • Weak forces → gases
Key Idea: The state of matter depends on both particle motion and intermolecular forces.

Interactive simulation: observe how gas particles move and respond to temperature changes.

Challenge Problem

Why are gases easily compressed while liquids are not?

Gas particles are far apart with large empty spaces, allowing compression. Liquid particles are closer together, so compression is limited.

Key Terms

  • Particle: Small unit of matter
  • Kinetic Energy: Energy of motion
  • Intermolecular Forces: Forces between particles
