Calculating Average Atomic Mass

Learning Objectives

  • Understand isotopic abundance
  • Calculate average atomic mass using weighted averages
  • Interpret atomic mass values from the periodic table

1. What is Average Atomic Mass?

Most elements exist as a mixture of isotopes. Because of this, the atomic mass shown on the periodic table is not a whole number—it is a weighted average.

Key Idea: Average atomic mass = sum of (isotope mass × fractional abundance)

Abundance must be expressed as a decimal (not percent).

2. Formula

Average Atomic Mass =
(m₁ × f₁) + (m₂ × f₂) + (m₃ × f₃) + ...

  • m = isotopic mass
  • f = fractional abundance (decimal form)

3. Step-by-Step Example

Problem:

An element has two isotopes:
Isotope A: mass = 10 amu, abundance = 20%
Isotope B: mass = 12 amu, abundance = 80%

Step 1: Convert % to decimal

  • 20% → 0.20
  • 80% → 0.80

Step 2: Multiply

  • 10 × 0.20 = 2.0
  • 12 × 0.80 = 9.6

Step 3: Add

2.0 + 9.6 = 11.6 amu

Concept Check

Why is atomic mass usually not a whole number?

Because it is a weighted average of isotopes.

Practice Drills

An element has:
Mass = 24 amu (75%)
Mass = 26 amu (25%)
Find the average atomic mass.

(24 × 0.75) + (26 × 0.25) = 18 + 6.5 = 24.5 amu

An element has three isotopes:
10 amu (50%), 11 amu (30%), 12 amu (20%)

(10×0.5)+(11×0.3)+(12×0.2)=5+3.3+2.4= 10.7 amu

Checkpoint (Challenge)

An element has:
Isotope X: 35 amu (60%)
Isotope Y: 37 amu (40%)

1. Calculate the average atomic mass.
2. Which isotope contributes more to the average and why?

(35×0.6)+(37×0.4)=21+14.8= 35.8 amu

Isotope X contributes more because it has higher abundance.
