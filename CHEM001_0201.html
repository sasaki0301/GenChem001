Writing Isotopic Symbol and Ionic Notation

Learning Objectives

  • Understand atomic number and mass number
  • Write isotopic symbols correctly
  • Represent ions using proper notation

1. Structure of the Atom

Atoms are composed of three main particles:

  • Protons: positive charge
  • Neutrons: no charge
  • Electrons: negative charge
Key Idea: The atomic number identifies the element, while the mass number represents total protons and neutrons.

2. Isotopic Symbol

An isotopic symbol is written as:

AXZ

  • A = mass number (protons + neutrons)
  • Z = atomic number (protons)

Example

Carbon-12:
A = 12, Z = 6
→ ¹²₆C

3. Step-by-Step Writing

Problem: Write the isotopic symbol for an atom with:

  • 8 protons
  • 10 neutrons

Step 1: Atomic number = 8 → Oxygen (O)

Step 2: Mass number = 8 + 10 = 18

Final: ¹⁸₈O

4. Ionic Notation

Ions form when atoms gain or lose electrons.

  • Lose electrons → positive ion (cation)
  • Gain electrons → negative ion (anion)

Na → Na⁺ (lost 1 electron)
Cl → Cl⁻ (gained 1 electron)

Key Idea: Charge = protons − electrons
