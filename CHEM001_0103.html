1. What Are Properties of Matter?

Properties of matter are characteristics that can be observed or measured to describe a substance. These properties help us identify, classify, and understand materials.

Key Idea: Properties describe how matter behaves and how it can be identified.

Copper has distinct properties such as color, conductivity, and malleability.

Quick Check

Is color a property of matter?

Yes, color is a physical property.

2. Physical vs Chemical Properties

Properties of matter can be classified into physical and chemical properties.

  • Physical properties: observed without changing composition (e.g., color, density)
  • Chemical properties: describe how a substance reacts (e.g., flammability)
Key Idea: Physical properties do not change identity, chemical properties involve transformation.

Understanding physical vs chemical properties and changes.

Concept Check

Is burning wood a physical or chemical property?

Chemical, because burning produces new substances like ash and gases.

3. Intensive vs Extensive Properties

Another way to classify properties is based on whether they depend on the amount of substance.

  • Intensive properties: do not depend on amount (e.g., density, temperature)
  • Extensive properties: depend on amount (e.g., mass, volume)
Key Idea: Intensive properties identify substances, while extensive properties describe quantity.

Explore how density remains constant regardless of amount.

Challenge Problem

Why is density considered an intensive property even when mass and volume change?

Because density is a ratio of mass to volume, and this ratio remains constant for a given substance.
