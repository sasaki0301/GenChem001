Chapter Summary: Atomic Structure and Isotopes

Big Idea

Atoms are identified by their number of protons, while isotopes differ in their number of neutrons. Ions form when atoms gain or lose electrons. The atomic mass shown on the periodic table is a weighted average of naturally occurring isotopes.

1. Atomic Number and Mass Number

  • Atomic number (Z): number of protons
  • Mass number (A): number of protons + neutrons
  • Neutrons: mass number − atomic number

Example

For ²³₁₁Na:
Protons = 11
Neutrons = 23 − 11 = 12

2. Isotopic Symbols

An isotopic symbol shows the mass number, atomic number, and element symbol.

AXZ

  • A = mass number
  • Z = atomic number
  • X = element symbol

Example: carbon-14 is written as ¹⁴₆C.

3. Isotopes

Isotopes are atoms of the same element with different numbers of neutrons.

  • Same number of protons
  • Same atomic number
  • Different mass number
  • Different number of neutrons

Example

¹²₆C and ¹⁴₆C are isotopes because both are carbon atoms, but they have different mass numbers.

4. Ionic Notation

Ions form when atoms gain or lose electrons.

  • Cation: positive ion formed by losing electrons
  • Anion: negative ion formed by gaining electrons
Charge = protons − electrons

Example

An atom with 13 protons and 10 electrons has a charge of:
13 − 10 = 3+
So it forms Al³⁺.

5. Average Atomic Mass

The atomic mass on the periodic table is usually not a whole number because it is a weighted average of all naturally occurring isotopes.

Average atomic mass = Σ(isotope mass × fractional abundance)

Remember: percent abundance must be converted to decimal form.

Example

Isotope A: 10 amu at 20%
Isotope B: 12 amu at 80%

Average atomic mass =
(10 × 0.20) + (12 × 0.80)
= 2.0 + 9.6
= 11.6 amu

Common Mistakes

  • Using percent abundance directly instead of decimal form
  • Confusing atomic number with mass number
  • Forgetting that ions differ by electrons, not protons
  • Thinking isotopes are different elements
  • Using simple average instead of weighted average

Quick Mastery Check

How many neutrons are in ³⁵₁₇Cl?

35 − 17 = 18 neutrons

An ion has 20 protons and 18 electrons. What is its charge?

20 − 18 = 2+

Calculate the average atomic mass: 24 amu at 75% and 26 amu at 25%.

(24 × 0.75) + (26 × 0.25) = 18 + 6.5 = 24.5 amu
