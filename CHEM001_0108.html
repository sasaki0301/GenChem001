Chapter 1 Summary: Matter and Measurement

Big Idea

Matter is composed of tiny particles whose motion, arrangement, and interactions determine the properties and behavior of substances. Accurate measurement allows us to describe and analyze these properties scientifically.

1. Particle Nature of Matter

  • All matter is made of particles (atoms, molecules, ions)
  • Particles are always in motion
  • Temperature affects particle motion

2. States of Matter and Phase Changes

  • Solids: fixed shape and volume
  • Liquids: fixed volume, variable shape
  • Gases: no fixed shape or volume
  • Phase changes involve energy transfer
During phase changes, temperature remains constant because energy is used to overcome intermolecular forces.

3. Properties of Matter

  • Physical: observed without changing identity
  • Chemical: involve reactions
  • Intensive: independent of amount
  • Extensive: depend on amount

4. Classification of Matter

  • Pure substances → elements and compounds
  • Mixtures → homogeneous and heterogeneous

5. Measurement and SI Units

  • SI units standardize measurements
  • Common units: meter, kilogram, second, kelvin
  • Dimensional analysis converts units

6. Accuracy, Precision, and Error

  • Accuracy = closeness to true value
  • Precision = consistency
  • All measurements have uncertainty

7. Significant Figures and Scientific Notation

  • Significant figures show measurement precision
  • Scientific notation expresses very large/small numbers

Common Mistakes

  • Confusing accuracy with precision
  • Incorrect use of significant figures
  • Forgetting unit conversions
  • Misclassifying mixtures and compounds
