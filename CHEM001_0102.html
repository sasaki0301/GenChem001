1. The Three Common States of Matter

Matter exists in different physical states depending on how particles are arranged, how strongly they interact, and how much energy they possess.

Key Idea: The state of matter depends on particle arrangement, motion, and intermolecular forces.

Comparison of particle arrangement in solids, liquids, and gases.

  • Solid: fixed shape and volume, particles tightly packed
  • Liquid: fixed volume but no fixed shape
  • Gas: no fixed shape or volume, particles move freely

2. Phase Changes

A phase change occurs when a substance transitions from one state of matter to another due to changes in energy.

Key Idea: Phase changes involve energy transfer, not a change in substance identity.
  • Melting: solid → liquid
  • Freezing: liquid → solid
  • Vaporization: liquid → gas
  • Condensation: gas → liquid

Concept Check

Why does temperature remain constant during boiling?

Energy is used to overcome intermolecular forces rather than increase kinetic energy.

3. Heating Curve and Energy Changes

When a substance is heated, its temperature does not always increase steadily. During phase changes, energy is absorbed without a rise in temperature.

Key Idea: During phase changes, energy changes potential energy, not kinetic energy.
