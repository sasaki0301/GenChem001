1. What Are Significant Figures?

Significant figures (sig figs) represent the precision of a measurement. They include all certain digits plus one estimated digit.

Key Idea: Significant figures reflect the reliability of a measurement.
  • Nonzero digits are always significant
  • Zeros between digits are significant
  • Leading zeros are NOT significant
  • Trailing zeros may or may not be significant

2. Rounding and Calculations

When performing calculations, the number of significant figures must be considered.

  • Add/Subtract: least number of decimal places
  • Multiply/Divide: least number of significant figures
Key Idea: The result of a calculation cannot be more precise than the least precise measurement.

How to apply significant figures in calculations.

Concept Check

Calculate: 2.5 × 3.42

2.5 × 3.42 = 8.55 → 2 significant figures → 8.6

3. Scientific Notation

Scientific notation is used to express very large or very small numbers in a compact form.

Key Idea: Scientific notation expresses numbers as a coefficient × 10ⁿ.

Example:
4500 = 4.5 × 10³
0.00032 = 3.2 × 10⁻⁴
